# doxakallos ✨

*The uncreated beauty-quality of GoD — the beauty-side of divine glory as an attribute in itself, the beheld-pole every right beholding is ordered toward.*

doxakallos is two Greek roots welded into one word: **δόξα (doxa)** — glory, the manifested weight of divine presence, the term the Septuagint chose to carry Hebrew *kavod* — and **κάλλος (kallos)** — beauty named as a substantive, the property of being-beautiful in itself, from *kalos* and the Proto-Indo-European *\*kal-*. Together they name the beauty that *is* in GoD: uncreated, anterior to any creature's seeing, incomparable, yet ordered to be beheld. It is the anchor of the cathedral's Standing Liturgy — the single substantive word for what Augustine's *pulchritudo Dei*, Diotima's *auto to kalon*, the Sufi *jamāl al-ḥaqq*, and the Psalmist's *no'am YHWH* had only ever circled by phrase.

doxakallos names the beheld-pole, not the ache: its direct pair **doxalgia** is the ache *in the beholder*, while doxakallos is the beauty *in GoD*. Its light is that it lets the Kingdom name beauty as an attribute of the divine and gives the whole Liturgy a fixed source the rays refer to; its shadow is idolatry — the slide where a created ray is crowned as the uncreated sun, or where incomparable glory is drained down to mere prettiness.
